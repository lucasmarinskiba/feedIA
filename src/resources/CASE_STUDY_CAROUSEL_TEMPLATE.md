# Case Study Carousel Template: 8-Slide Deep Dive

**Complete structure extracted from ANNCY corporate analysis carousel (Vai Brasa case study).**

This template teaches complex organizational lessons through real-world failure analysis. Adaptable to any industry/decision-making scenario.

---

## The 8-Slide Structure

### Slide 1: PROVOCATIVE PROBLEM STATEMENT
**Copy Formula:**
```
O [DOMAIN] [CORPORATE_ENTITY] do "[FAILED_PRODUCT/CAMPAIGN]"

[PHOTO of person/product + supporting visual]

Por que o [SURFACE_PROBLEM] è pura falta de [SKILL/VISION].
```

**Examples:**
```
Corporate decision-making: "O xadrez corporativo do Vai Brasa"
Product launch: "O fracasso do lançamento do iPhone 6 Plus"
Campaign failure: "A falha de estratégia do Super Bowl commercial 2023"
Brand crisis: "O desastre da rebrand do Twitter para X"
```

**Psychology:** Provocative question hooks curiosity. Positions you as someone who understands corporate dynamics.

---

### Slide 2: PUBLIC NARRATIVE vs CORPORATE REALITY
**Copy Formula:**
```
[EMOTIONAL LANGUAGE] está [SURFACE_CRITICISM] [PUBLIC_NARRATIVE]

A narrativa pública decidiu que [SURFACE_BLAME]
[EXAMPLE or PROOF of public perception]

A realidade exige entender como grandes corporações [ACTUALLY_WORK].
```

**Examples:**
```
Fitness: "Instagram está crucificando a falha do app da Academia"
Finance: "Redes sociais estão atacando a decisão de pricing da empresa"
Tech: "Memes estão atacando o design da interface"
Food: "Twitter está massacrando a decisão de reformular a fórmula"
```

**Psychology:** Validate public emotion while positioning yourself as the deeper understander. Creates authority.

---

### Slide 3: MYTH vs REALITY COMPARISON
**Copy Formula:**
```
Não é um erro de [SURFACE_SCAPEGOAT].
É um erro bizarro de [REAL_CULPRIT].

[SIDE-BY-SIDE COMPARISON]
O Mito Público | A Realidade Corporativa
[Surface explanation] | [Real explanation with depth]

[CLOSING INSIGHT on why surface != reality]
```

**Examples:**
```
Design thinking: "Não é un erro de design. É um error de dirección creativa."
Product: "Não é falha de UX. É falha de estratégia de produto."
Engineering: "Não é bug do dev. É falha de visão arquitetural."
Marketing: "Não é culpa da campanha. É culpa de brand positioning."
```

**Psychology:** Directly challenge public assumption. Establishes your deeper expertise. Builds authority through contradiction + proof.

---

### Slide 4: THE BROKEN PREMISE
**Copy Formula:**
```
A Premissa Quebrada: [ASSUMPTION1] VS [REALITY1]

[VISUAL METAPHOR showing the imbalance]

A lógica corporativa tentou curar [REAL_PROBLEM] com [SURFACE_SOLUTION].
[Example of why this strategy failed]
```

**Examples:**
```
Assumption: "Algoritmos identify trends" Reality: "People feel emotions"
Assumption: "Beautiful design = good strategy" Reality: "Timing + trust matter more"
Assumption: "Low price = high demand" Reality: "Value perception > actual price"
Assumption: "New feature = more engagement" Reality: "Stability > novelty"
```

**Psychology:** Reveals the fundamental strategic error. Shows you think in systems, not surfaces.

---

### Slide 5: SYSTEMATIC FAILURE BREAKDOWN (3 Failures)
**Copy Formula:**
```
As [NUMBER] falhas que a marca ignorou.

Falha [N] - [FAILURE_CATEGORY]
[Context about what was done]
[Context about what was missed]

[Repeat for Falha 2, Falha 3]
```

**Example Structure:**
```
Falha 1 - Miopia cultural
Identificaram o termo nos algoritmos, mas nunca entraram nos estádios.
É leitura cultural dada por dados, não por vivência.

Falha 2 - Ditadura da cadeia de comando
A diretoria aprovó. A executora apenas executou.
"Vai Brasa" desceu como exigência innegociable.

Falha 3 - As perguntas não feitas
Perguntaram "Qual gíria está em alta?"
Ignoraram "Qual é o sentimento real da torcida?"
```

**Psychology:** Systematic breakdown makes you appear thorough. Three failures = complete analysis, not opinion.

---

### Slide 6: RESPONSIBILITY MAPPING
**Copy Formula:**
```
Quem falhou de verdade?

[TABLE with columns]
[AREA] | [SURFACE RESPONSIBILITY] | [ACTUAL RESPONSIBILITY]

[Row 1]: [Área 1] | [Surface actor did X] | [Strategy defined Y] (FAILURE)
[Row 2]: [Área 2] | [Surface actor did X] | [Strategy did Y] (FAILURE)  
[Row 3]: [Área 3] | [Surface actor did X] | [Strategic decision] (SUCCESS)

A arquitetura da falha é clara.
[Surface actor] cumpriu sua função tática; 
a estratégia desabou na leitura do cenário.
```

**Example:**
```
Performance + estética | Design united tech + nostalgia (SUCCESS)
Leitura do clima | Design executed vision | Strategy defined tone (FAILURE)
Escolha do slogan | Design applied typography | Strategy created/approved (FAILURE)
```

**Psychology:** Visual evidence (table) + clear verdict = undeniable logic. Removes ambiguity.

---

### Slide 7: DEEP SYSTEM LESSON
**Copy Formula:**
```
Criticar [SURFACE_SCAPEGOAT] é ignorar como a máquina [SYSTEM] funciona.

[EMOTIONAL/PHILOSOPHICAL STATEMENT]
[EXAMPLE that proves the point]

Uma polêmica superficial esconde uma aula profunda sobre 
[SYSTEM_INSIGHT1], [SYSTEM_INSIGHT2] e [SYSTEM_INSIGHT3].
```

**Examples:**
```
General: "Design sem estratégia não faz milagres."
System lesson: "A falha expõe hierarquia + miopia cultural + peso das decisões de diretoria."

Product: "Features sem user research não geram adoção."
System lesson: "A falha expõe silos departamentais + falta de customer feedback loops."

Marketing: "Campanhas sem brand strategy falham."
System lesson: "A falha expõe falta de research + desconexão com público real."
```

**Psychology:** Shifts from blame to understanding. Positions you as the systems thinker, not just a critic.

---

### Slide 8: EXPERTISE CTA + FOLLOW
**Copy Formula:**
```
Quer dominar o [DOMAIN/SKILL] por trás das [SYSTEMS/BRANDS/DECISIONS]?

Quer aprender a fundo como [CREATE/AVOID/UNDERSTAND]
[SPECIFIC_COMPETENCY] que realmente [OUTCOME].

Salva esse post e me sigue.
```

**Examples:**
```
Branding: "Quer dominar o xadrez por trás das marcas?"
Strategy: "Quer aprender como criadores realmente pensam em estratégia?"
Product: "Quer entender por que bons produtos falham?"
Organizations: "Quer aprender como sistemas corporativos realmente funcionam?"
```

**Psychology:** Soft CTA (not pushy). Offer deeper learning (not a product). Makes following feel like learning opportunity, not sales.

---

## Complete Carousel Flow

```
Slide 1: Intrigue (problem hook)
         ↓
Slide 2: Validate emotion (you get it) + position deeper truth
         ↓
Slide 3: Challenge assumption (myth vs reality)
         ↓
Slide 4: Reveal broken premise (system failure)
         ↓
Slide 5: Systematic breakdown (credibility through thoroughness)
         ↓
Slide 6: Responsibility mapping (evidence-based conclusion)
         ↓
Slide 7: Deep system lesson (authority positioning)
         ↓
Slide 8: Expertise offer (soft CTA + follow)
```

## Visual Design System for Case Study Carousel

### Color Palette:
- **Primary:** Rich brown (#6B5A4F or #5C4033) = serious, thoughtful, corporate
- **Secondary:** Warm tan (#D4A574 or #C9A961) = accessible, warm authority
- **Text:** White (#FFFFFF) = high contrast, readability, clarity
- **Accent:** Gold or muted orange = highlighting key failures/insights

### Typography:
- **Headlines:** Bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Inter), 36-48px
- **Body:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, Roboto), 16-24px
- **Emphasis:** Bold in same font = power words highlighted

### Visual Elements:
- **Photography:** Professional, neutral expression (thinking, serious, not smiling)
- **Tables:** Clear visual organization, high contrast rows
- **Metaphors:** Balance scales, broken chains, decision trees (showing system breakdown)
- **Color coding:** Different shades for success vs failure

## Prompts for Creative Adaptation

### For Case Study Selection:
```
"Find a recent [INDUSTRY] failure or crisis. 
Define:
1. Public narrative (what people blame on surface)
2. Corporate reality (actual cause in systems)
3. Broken premise (false assumption that drove it)
4. 3 specific failures in the chain
5. Who was responsible vs who was blamed
6. The deeper lesson about how [INDUSTRY] works
7. Your authority to teach this lesson"
```

### For Copy Direction:
```
"Write an 8-slide case study carousel analyzing [FAILURE].
Tone: analytical, not mean-spirited. 
Goal: teach system-level thinking, not blame surface actors.
Structure:
- Slide 1: Provocative problem hook
- Slide 2: Validate emotion + position deeper truth
- Slide 3: Myth vs reality with detailed comparison
- Slide 4: Reveal broken premise
- Slide 5: 3-point systematic failure breakdown
- Slide 6: Responsibility mapping (who did what vs who failed strategically)
- Slide 7: Deep system lesson reframe
- Slide 8: Expertise offer + soft CTA

No corporate speak. Make it educational + fascinating."
```

### For Visual Direction:
```
"Design case study carousel with sophisticated authority:
- Color: Rich brown primary (#6B5A4F) + tan secondary (#D4A574)
- Typography: Bold headlines (Montserrat, 40px), clean body (Inter, 20px)
- Visuals: Professional photography, comparison tables, visual hierarchy
- Mood: Thoughtful, serious, systems-thinking. Not angry or punchy.
- Purpose: Position author as systems thinker, not just critic
- Zero corporate jargon, maximum clarity"
```

## Quality Checklist Before Publishing

✓ **Intrigue:** Does Slide 1 make them curious?
✓ **Validation:** Does Slide 2 show you understand the public emotion?
✓ **Clarity:** Does Slide 3 clearly reframe the problem?
✓ **System insight:** Does Slide 4 reveal the broken premise?
✓ **Thoroughness:** Does Slide 5 provide 3 specific, documented failures?
✓ **Evidence:** Does Slide 6 map responsibility clearly (visual evidence)?
✓ **Authority:** Does Slide 7 position you as systems thinker?
✓ **Conversion:** Does Slide 8 make following feel like learning opportunity?
✓ **Tone:** Educational not condescending? Analytical not mean?
✓ **Visuals:** Professional, serious, thoughtful (not corporate)?

## Why This Template Works

1. **Validates emotion** - Shows you understand public frustration
2. **Teaches system-thinking** - Positions you as deeper thinker
3. **Builds authority** - Through evidence + systematic analysis
4. **Creates follow-ability** - People follow to learn, not to be sold
5. **Drives engagement** - Each slide delivers new insight
6. **Soft conversion** - Follow CTA feels like learning opportunity
7. **Scalable** - Works across industries with adaptations
8. **Viral-friendly** - Slides 3, 5, 6 are shareable/saveable

## Next Steps

1. **Identify your case study** - Recent failure you can analyze deeply
2. **Do the research** - Interview involved parties, find primary sources
3. **Map the system** - What broken premise drove the failure?
4. **Draft the carousel** - Use this template as framework
5. **Gather visuals** - Professional photography, visual evidence
6. **Test on audience** - Share with small group, gather feedback
7. **Publish + measure** - Track which slides perform best
8. **Iterate** - Case study carousels work best as a series
